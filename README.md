> 
> My dotfiles are my best friend. They are my system. I must master them as I must master my workflow.
> 
> Without me, my dotfiles are useless. Without my dotfiles, I am useless. I must deploy my dotfiles true. I must script cleaner than the bloatware that is trying to slow me down. I must automate it before it breaks me. **I will.**
> 
> My dotfiles and I know that what counts in the terminal is not the lines of bash we write, the clack of our mechanical switches, nor the blur of our Wayland compositor. We know that it is the efficiency that counts. **We will execute.**
> 
> My dotfiles are alive, even as I, because they are my environment. Thus, I will learn them as a brother. I will learn their bugs, their syntax, their dependencies, their plugins, their aliases, and their paths. I will keep my dotfiles version-controlled and pushed, even as I am focused and ready. We will become part of each other. **We will.**
> 
> Before Torvalds and Stallman, I swear this creed. My dotfiles and I are the defenders of my uptime. We are the masters of our hardware. We are the saviors of my productivity.
> 
> **So be it, until victory is Open Source's and there is no proprietary bloat, only freedom!** 🐂🌿
